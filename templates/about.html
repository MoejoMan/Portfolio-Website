About Me

My name is Joseph Hayes Gonzalez. Computers have always fascinated me, and somewhere along the way that curiosity turned into a passion for cybersecurity. I sit on the blue team side of things mostly — figuring out how to keep systems safe, building practical tools, and spreading awareness about safe internet practices.

I learn by doing. From turning a Raspberry Pi Pico into a USB Rubber Ducky, to building an SSH brute-force analyzer, to running my own WireGuard VPN and SIEM on cloud infrastructure — I'm always looking for the next thing to build or break (responsibly). I run a homelab out of my uni dorm with Cisco switches, a refurbished Dell OptiPlex server, and cloud VPS instances.

Down the line, I want to work somewhere I can make a real impact — whether that's in a SOC, a security consultancy, or a role where I'm defending critical infrastructure. I'm eager to get hands-on industry experience.
